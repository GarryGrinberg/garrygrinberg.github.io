🎯 From Shopping Mall to Mansion

When You Know Numerology, You Can Network Anywhere

Me and my wife were shopping last weekend— she was taking her time picking out clothes.

While she browsed, she was using the cue app I developed that helps you choose clothes based on astrology.

(It actually saved us quite a bit of time—worth checking out.)

I spotted another bored husband waiting by the fitting rooms. We got talking about

sports. I mentioned how Tiger Woods and LeBron James share the same birthday—December

30th. Both champions, both 28 lifepaths. (12/30/1975 and 12/30/1984)

He asked for a reading. Five minutes later, he looked at me like I'd just read his entire

life story.

Turns out Mark (not his real name) works in entertainment. High-level stuff. He invited

me to a party that weekend at his boss's place. "They will love you"

I went home, got fitted for a proper suit, and showed up ready.

🚪 The Bouncer Who Changed Everything

When my Uber Black pulled up to the mansion, I knew this wasn't just any party. Security at

the gate. Valet parking. The whole setup.

The bouncer checked his list. My name wasn't on it. He was about to turn me away.

"What's your birthday?" I asked him.

He looked skeptical. "July 4th."

"Let me guess—off-duty cop?"

His eyes went wide. "Wow, how did you know?"

I smiled. "You're a 4 energy. Structure, discipline, protection, justice. The 4th of July

isn't just your birthday—it's your life path. You're drawn to serve and protect. It's in

your numbers."

He stepped aside and waved me through.

How Personal Numbers Work

Birth dates reveal career paths, relationship patterns, and even personality traits.

People born on the 4th, 13th, 22nd, or 31st all carry 4 energy—they're natural

protectors, builders, and authority figures.

Understanding these patterns lets you connect with anyone, anywhere.

🎉 When the Whole Party Wanted Readings

Mark found me inside and introduced me to his boss—someone you'd definitely recognize if I

could say their name. A-list celebrity. Multiple industries.

I didn't pitch anything. Just asked for a birthday and shared what the numbers showed.

Three people became five. Five became nine. Nine became the entire party crowded around me,

shouting birth dates, laughing at how accurate everything was.

I wasn't trying to be the center of attention. But when you can show people patterns

they've felt but never understood, they come to you.
